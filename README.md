## 📊 Credit Card Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)

## 🔍 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing credit card transactions and customer behavior using Power BI. The goal is to derive actionable insights related to revenue, spending patterns, customer segmentation, and transaction trends.

The project consists of two interactive dashboards:
1. Credit Card Transaction Report
2. Credit Card Customer Report

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze overall revenue and transaction trends
- Understand customer demographics and behavior
- Identify high-value customer segments
- Track spending patterns across categories
- Provide insights for business decision-making

## 📁 Dataset Description
The dataset includes multiple tables:
- credit_card – transaction details
- customer – customer demographic information
- cc_add – additional card-related data
- cust_add – extended customer attributes

### Key Fields:
- Revenue
- Transaction Amount 
- Interest Earned
- Card Category (Blue, Silver, Gold, Platinum)
- Customer Job, Education, Income Group
- Transaction Type (Swipe, Chip, Online)
- Week Start Date

## 📊 Dashboard 1: Credit Card Transaction Report
### Key KPIs:
- 💰 Total Revenue: 55.3M
- 💳 Transaction Amount: 45M
- 📈 Total Interest: 7.8M
- 🔢 Transaction Count: 656K

### Key Insights:
- Blue card category contributes the highest revenue (~46M)
- Majority transactions are done via Swipe method
- Q3 shows peak revenue and transaction volume
- Top spending categories:
      - Bills
      - Entertainment
      - Fuel
         
### Visualizations:
- Revenue & Transaction Trend (Quarter-wise)
- Revenue by Card Category
- Revenue by Expenditure Type
- Revenue by Customer Job
- Revenue by Education Level

## 📊 Dashboard 2: Credit Card Customer Report
### Key KPIs:
- 💰 Revenue: 55M
- 💼 Income: 576M
- 📈 Interest: 8M
- 📊 Customer Satisfaction Score (CSS): 3.19

### Key Insights:
- Male customers generate higher revenue than female customers
- Businessman and White-collar jobs contribute the most revenue
- Customers aged 40–60 are the highest contributors
- High-income group drives the majority of revenue
- Married customers contribute slightly more than single customers

### Visualizations:
- Revenue by Age Group
- Revenue by Gender
- Revenue by Income Group
- Revenue by Marital Status
- Revenue by State (Top 5)
- Weekly Revenue Trend
